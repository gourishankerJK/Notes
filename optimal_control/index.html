Contents

Seven Chapters
  1. I Introduction The optimal control problem, finite-dimensional optimization background, and a first preview of infinite-dimensional methods. 4 sections
  2. II Calculus of Variations Classical variational problems, the Euler–Lagrange equation, Hamiltonian formalism and mechanics, constraints, and second-order conditions. 7 sections
  3. III From Calculus of Variations to Optimal Control Strong extrema, the Weierstrass conditions, and the conceptual bridge from classical variational methods to modern optimal control. 5 sections
  4. IV The Maximum Principle Pontryagin's maximum principle: full statement, a complete proof, time-optimal control, and the existence of optimal controls. 6 sections
  5. V The Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman Equation Dynamic programming and the HJB equation, its relationship to the maximum principle, and viscosity solutions. 4 sections
  6. VI The Linear Quadratic Regulator Finite- and infinite-horizon LQR, the Riccati differential equation, and closed-loop stability of the optimal feedback law. 3 sections
  7. VII Advanced Topics The maximum principle on manifolds, symplectic geometry, Riccati inequalities in robust control, and hybrid control systems. 5 sections
